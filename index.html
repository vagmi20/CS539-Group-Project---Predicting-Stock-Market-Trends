Project Overview

This project explores the potential of integrating historical stock performance data with real-time news sentiment to predict future stock movements. Our aim is to provide investors with a tool to assess the impact of news sentiment on stock prices, thereby supporting informed investment decisions.

Data Collection and Processing

Stock Data

We collected two years of stock data for Apple, NVIDIA, and Meta from Yahoo Finance. The attributes captured include Date, Adjusted Close, Open, High, Low, Close, and Volume. Below is a sample of this dataset:

News Data

News data was gathered using Google News queries, which extracted articles containing information on these companies. We utilized BeautifulSoup and URLLib3 for web scraping, as NewsAPI provided only limited text snippets.

Sentiment Analysis

Each news article was analyzed to extract sentiment (negative, neutral, positive), which was then used to augment our stock data for predictive modeling.

Feature Engineering and Model Training

From these datasets we were able to gather 6 features from the daily sentiment analysis- the number of articles by sentiment class(negative,neutral, positive) and the mean probability by sentiment class(negative, neutral, positive). In total we got 7 stock features in addition to these 6 features from the sentiment analysis.

For the labels, we compared on day's adjusted close price to the following day's adjusted close price. If the next day price is greater we assign a label of 1(upward trend) otherwise, we assign a label of 0(downward trend).

Using our features from the sentiment and stock data and our generated labels, we were able to run 8 machine learning models which we review in the next section.

Finally, to output the results of these models we used an Ensemble method which would iterate over a dictionary of models, train them with predefined parameters, and evaluates them using the test dataset. It then identifies the best-performing model based on the highest accuracy score from the stored results. At the end of this method, it prints a comprehensive overviews of all tested models, listing their accuracies for a quick comparison.

Conclusions and Future Work

Based on our results from these models tested on three different companies, we determined that the SVM classifier and the Neural Network are the most accurate at being able to determine if a stock is likely to have an upward or downward trend. In the future, to improve these models, we should include more news data as well as account for bias in the articles. Although we only tested these models for three companies, we could expand the scope and look into other companies as well to get a more comprehensive overview of how stock data is changing over time and in the future.
